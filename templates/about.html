Why YSP matters.

A note from founder, Mei Tong K.

Young people are among those most profoundly affected by the policy decisions being made today — on climate, economic systems, digital governance, and public health — yet they are rarely given accessible entry points to understand or influence those decisions.

YSP exists to close that gap. It does so not by simplifying policies, but by equipping young people with the research skills, the writing practice, the network, and the published track record that allow them to enter policy spaces with genuine credibility.

The byline, the certificates, the mentor relationship, and the alumni network are not afterthoughts — they are mechanisms through which YSP converts programme participation into career-relevant evidence that members can point to for the rest of their professional lives.

YSP's Four Mechanisms

How we convert participation
into evidence that lasts.

Every YSP member finishes a cohort with four things they can point to for the rest of their career — not as keepsakes, but as the working proof that the work was real.

01 The byline.

Every Impact Project is published under the member's own name — researched, drafted, and signed. The byline is the most portable credential we offer.

Published work
02 The certificate.

A signed track-completion certificate, issued by YSP and recognised across our partner institutions. Plain, verifiable, and earned.

Verifiable credential
03 The mentor.

A practitioner mentor walks alongside every member across the 16-week curriculum. Most relationships outlast the cohort.

Long-form relationship
04 The network.

An alumni database that opens up referrals, collaborations, and introductions across research, policy, and civic institutions in the region.

Alumni network
What YSP Is

A small, careful team
doing patient work.

YSP is youth-led, practitioner-guided, and built on the belief that good policy work requires patience, evidence, and people who genuinely care about the outcome.

Founded 2024

Youth-led, by design.

Founded by a team of students and early-career practitioners who wanted policy work to be something young people could enter — not just read about. The C-Suite is youth; the advisor bench is senior.

Free · No fees

Open to anyone who shows up.

Membership is free and always will be. There are no application fees, no hidden tracks, and no prerequisites — just a willingness to do the work seriously, and a pledge to do it with care.

Global · ASEAN-rooted

Regional in focus, global in reach.

YSP is grounded in Singapore and Southeast Asia, but our network spans 16+ countries. Members work on issues that matter regionally — and meet collaborators from far beyond it.
