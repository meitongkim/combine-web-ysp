01 ─ Flagship Applications open

Policy Studios

Six‑month studios on housing, climate adaptation, and digital rights. Pair with a senior advisor, draft a brief, ship a pilot.

Twelve fellows per pillar, three pillars per cohort. You spend the first eight weeks inside the literature and the people doing the work, the next eight building, and the last eight pushing the output into a room where it matters. Output is whatever the question demands — usually a policy brief, sometimes a working pilot, occasionally model legislation. Everything ships under your name.

02 ─ Field Summer · regional

Field Fellowships

Embed with a city office, a non‑profit, or a regulator for ten weeks. Stipend included.

Fellowships are how you find out whether the brief you wrote actually meets the desk it was meant for. You sit inside a host organisation, in the room where decisions take shape — drafting memos, sitting in on consultations, learning what gets cut and why. We place the cohort across four regions every summer and pair each fellow with a senior staffer on the host side and a YSP advisor outside it.

03 ─ Sprints Rolling

Brief Sprints

Two‑week intensives. One question, one brief, published openly.

Sprints are the smallest viable unit of policy work. A question lands on a Monday — usually one that's broken on a journalist's desk that week — and a brief is published the second Friday after. Eight fellows work in pairs, each pair on a different angle. A senior reviewer reads everything on day nine and the strongest three drafts go through final polish. The rest go in the working notebook.

LengthTwo weeks
Cohort8 fellows · 4 pairs
Time~12 hrs / week
StipendNo — open

04 ─ Rooms Online + four cities

Reading Rooms

Slow, monthly seminars on the literature behind today's policy debates.

The room is small on purpose. Twelve people, a chair, two papers, one conversation — the kind of seminar policy people had before everything became a panel. We pick the texts that the current debate is sitting on top of and read them properly. No slides, no talks, no agenda beyond a question the chair will refuse to let you off the hook for. London, Berlin, Nairobi, Singapore, and online.

05 ─ Open Year‑round

Open Studio

Bring your own initiative. We provide critique, contacts, and a desk.

Some of the best work walks in the door already half‑built — a campaign someone's been running, a paper that needs sharpening, a coalition looking for a brief. Open Studio is for those. We don't reshape what you're doing; we sit with it, ask the awkward questions, open the doors we can open, and lend the room and the people. Twelve seats held open all year. Roll in when you're ready.

How a studio runs

Same shape, different tempo.

Whatever the length — two weeks or six months — every studio follows the same six beats. Sprints compress them into a fortnight; flagships stretch them across half a year.

Beat 01 Frame

Land on the question. Strip it of jargon. Write the headline you'd want to see when the work is done.

Beat 02 Read

Cover the literature properly. Find the three pieces that quietly hold up the current debate.

Beat 03 Listen

Five conversations with people closer to the desk than you are. Notes shared with the cohort.

Beat 04 Draft

Write the brief. Cut it in half. Show it to your mentor. Cut it again. Show it to a stranger.

Beat 05 Test

Put it in front of someone who would actually use it. Take the awkward feedback. Revise.

Beat 06 Ship

Publish openly, under your name. Send it to the rooms it was written for. Stay in the conversation.

Questions, plainly

Things people actually ask.

How is a studio different from a route?+
A route is what you study — Research, Policy, Business, Diplomacy, Environment, Community. A studio is how you study it: the format, the cadence, the people in the room. Most fellows do a route once and pass through several studios.
Do I have to be a YSP fellow to apply?+
Flagship, Field, and Open Studio are fellows‑only. Brief Sprints and Reading Rooms are open to anyone 16–28; about half of every sprint cohort comes from outside YSP.
Is there a fee?+
No. YSP studios are free. Flagship and Field also pay a stipend — £2,400 and £4,800 respectively for 2026/27. Travel for in‑person Reading Rooms is reimbursed if cost is a barrier; ask, we mean it.
How competitive are flagships?+
The last cohort received 380 applications for 36 seats. We optimise for range, not pedigree: one in four fellows last cycle had no prior policy training. Show us a question you'd protect.
Can I run a studio at my university or city?+
Reading Rooms travel well — we've stood them up in eight cities so far on local invitation. Write to chapters@ysp.org with a chair, a venue, and a date that's at least eight weeks out.
What about time zones and language?+
Studios are predominantly async; the live touchpoints (mentor 1:1s, the chair‑led reading rooms) are scheduled per cohort. English is the working language but mentors are matched bilingually where we can — currently in French, Mandarin, Spanish, Arabic, Swahili, and Portuguese.
What happens to my work after a studio ends?+
You own it. We publish your final brief openly under your byline, with a YSP imprint credit. Sprint and flagship briefs become part of the YSP working notebook and are regularly cited in mainstream policy press.
